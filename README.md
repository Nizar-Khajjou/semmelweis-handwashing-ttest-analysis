# Semmelweis Handwashing Discovery: Statistical Analysis

A statistical analysis recreating Dr. Ignaz Semmelweis's famous 19th-century discovery — using a t-test to prove that handwashing significantly reduced childbed fever deaths in maternity clinics, at a time when the medical establishment rejected the idea germs caused disease.

## What it does
- Analyzes historical mortality data from Vienna General Hospital's maternity clinics
- Compares death rates before and after mandatory handwashing was introduced
- Runs an independent t-test to determine whether the observed drop in deaths was statistically significant or due to random chance
- Visualizes the death rate trend over time, highlighting the intervention point

## Key finding
The t-test confirms an extremely low p-value, providing strong statistical evidence that the reduction in deaths after handwashing was a real effect — not random variation — validating Semmelweis's discovery over a century after it was originally dismissed.
